About

At Bigship, a ₹100 Cr ARR logistics platform, I've been the sole owner of the entire product function — a 90K+ seller-facing platform spanning domestic, hyperlocal, and international shipping, and a 300-user operational ERP across 3 branches. That's not one product. It's an enterprise platform ecosystem: financial workflows, AI pipelines, lifecycle architecture, carrier integrations, and internal governance tooling — all built and shipped without a product team around me.

Before that, at Programming Pathshala, I owned the product function for an EdTech SaaS — built a B2B assessment platform and white-labeled LMS from zero (15+ university campuses, ₹24L+ ARR), discovered that the founding ICP was inverted through cohort analysis and repositioned the entire product around it, and ran a Fellowship Program that generated ₹1.5 Cr+ in its first quarter. Each was a different kind of problem. The thread was the same: figure out what's actually wrong, build something with real edges, ship it.

The problems I'm drawn to sit at the edge of product and systems — where a workflow is breaking because the data taxonomy is wrong, where AI is being shipped as a feature instead of designed as infrastructure, where a revenue model is trapped inside an ad hoc process that nobody has productised yet. I find those constraints and I build through them.

I started in teaching — 200+ sessions a month, learners from 5 to 60, every learning style and neurotype. That's where I learned to read a person faster than a research report can. The user instinct I use every day was built in a room, not a tool.

HOW I WORK
Design for the constraint beneath the constraint.
The first problem statement is rarely the right one. The data taxonomy is broken. The AI is a feature, not infrastructure. The workflow exists because nobody productised the exception. I build to that layer.
Redirect with evidence, not opinion.
Five founder decisions redirected — each one won by framing in their currency, not mine. Data, commercial case, timeline. Not preference.
Shipping is where reality begins.
Adoption is part of the spec. A product that shipped but isn't used hasn't shipped. I own the full arc — discovery through scaled execution.
Know when to stop.
I built Phil. Ran structured research. Found weak PMF. Recommended discontinuation. That call is worth more than the build — and harder to make.

"Long enough in product to know that shipping is never the finish — it's where reality begins. The real work is what survives after: reduced friction, sensible details, and earned trust."

— Divya Nautiyal
